Dining FAQ for Students

What is a meal plan?

A meal plan is a pre-paid account for on-campus dining purchases. Meal plans are set up at the beginning of each academic term and charged to the student's tuition
bill. Meal plans include a set number of meals and Viking Bucks loaded onto the student ID card.

Meal plans are non-transferable and cannot be used by others. Students must show photo ID when using their meal plan.

Breakfast is 7-10:30 a.m., lunch is 10:30 a.m.-4:30 p.m., and dinner is 4:30-8 p.m. Students can use up to two meal swipes per meal period.

What is a meal swipe?

Meal swipes can be used at the all-you-can-eat Gerber Center Dining Room or to purchase meal bundles at Gus' Snack Bar, Brew by the Slough, and Westerlin Market.

Meal swipes on the Any 80 and Any 100 meal plans roll over term-to-term during the academic year. Any unused meal swipes at the end of the academic year are 
forfeited.

What are Viking Bucks?

Viking Bucks are valued dollar-for-dollar and can be used at any Dining Services location on campus, including the Gerber Center Dining Room, Brew by the Slough, 
Westerlin Market, Gus' Snack Bar, and concession stands.

Viking Bucks roll over term-to-term during the academic year. Any remaining Viking Bucks at the end of the academic year are forfeited.

Who is required to have a meal plan?

All first-year students and sophomores are required to have an on-campus meal plan. Juniors, seniors, and commuter students may choose whether to purchase a meal 
plan.

How do I sign up for a meal plan?

First-year students and sophomores are automatically assigned the Any 15 plan. Meal plan changes must be made during the first two weeks of the term.

How can I check my meal plan account balance?

Students can ask a cashier at any dining location or contact the Dining Services Office.

Can I add additional Viking Bucks or meals?

Additional Viking Bucks or meals may be added at any time through the Dining Services Office.

What happens to unused meals and Viking Bucks?

Unused Viking Bucks and off-campus meal plan swipes roll over during the academic year but are forfeited at the end of the academic year. No refunds are given.

How much do meals cost at the Gerber Center Dining Room?

Breakfast (7-10:30 a.m.): $10.25

Lunch (10:30 a.m.-4:30 p.m.): $11.75

Dinner (4:30-8 p.m.): $11.75

Meals are all-you-can-eat. Carryout meals cost the same price.

What is continuous dining?

Continuous dining means hot meals are available throughout operating hours.

Is carryout available?

Students may create a to-go meal using a meal swipe, Viking Bucks, cash, credit/debit cards, or checks.

One to-go container is included with on-campus meal plans for first-year and transfer students. Additional containers may be purchased for $6.

How do I get a sick meal?

Students who cannot visit the dining hall may arrange for a friend to pick up a meal by contacting the Dining Services Office and providing their information.

What is a meal bundle?

Meal bundles generally include one entrée, two side dishes, and a drink.

Bundles are available at Westerlin Market, Gus' Snack Bar, and Brew by the Slough.

Entrées may include sandwiches, wraps, salads, yogurt parfaits, fruit trays, vegetable trays, and hot grilled items.

Side dishes may include pasta salad, cottage cheese, carrots, apple slices, fresh fruit, cheese, and chips.

Drinks include soda, bottled water, or milk.

Can friends join me in the dining room if they are not eating?

Only people who have purchased a meal may enter the dining room.

Where is the Dining Services Office?

The office is located in the Dining Room and is open Monday-Friday from 8 a.m.-4:30 p.m.

What if I have special dietary needs?

Dining locations offer gluten-free, vegetarian, and vegan options daily. Students may contact the Dining Services Office for additional assistance.
